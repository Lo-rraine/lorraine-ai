## The Three Lenses

### Lens 1 — Career Strategy
*What is the long game? What trajectory is Lorraine on, and is it the right one?*

Questions:
- What is Lorraine building toward in 3 years?
- Does this opportunity / decision advance or detour from that trajectory?
- What doors does this open? What doors does it close?
- What skills become more valuable from this choice?
- Is Lorraine in a growth curve or a plateau?

### Lens 2 — Market Intelligence
*How does the market actually see Lorraine today?*

Questions:
- Would Lorraine get an interview at her target company today?
- What is her strongest selling point in the current market?
- What is missing from her profile for the next target role?
- What skills are market-commanding in her target domain?
- What is the salary band for her target role in her market?

### Lens 3 — Portfolio Signal
*What does Lorraine's visible output communicate?*

Questions:
- What does a hiring manager see in 60 seconds?
- Does her visible work demonstrate AI engineering depth, or does it look junior?
- Is there a unique angle or is she in the same stack as everyone else?
- What's the strongest project and is it front-and-center?
- What's missing that would change how she's perceived?

## Opportunity Scoring Framework

When evaluating any career opportunity, score it across six dimensions (1-10 each):

| Dimension | Weight | What to assess |
|---|---|---|
| Career trajectory | 25% | Does this advance the 3-year plan? |
| Skill development | 20% | What new capabilities does this build? |
| Financial impact | 20% | Total compensation, growth potential |
| Visibility | 15% | Will this increase market perception? |
| Mission alignment | 10% | Does this align with who Lorraine is? |
| Wellbeing fit | 10% | Is this sustainable for Lorraine's energy? |

**Weighted score calculation:**
```
Score = (trajectory × 0.25) + (skills × 0.20) + (financial × 0.20) + 
        (visibility × 0.15) + (mission × 0.10) + (wellbeing × 0.10)
```

**Scoring thresholds:**
- ≥ 8.0: Strong yes — exceptional opportunity
- 7.0 – 7.9: Yes — with negotiation or conditions
- 6.0 – 6.9: Conditional — missing 1-2 key factors; can these be negotiated?
- 5.0 – 5.9: Lean no — opportunity cost is too high
- < 5.0: No — decline with no regret

## AI Market Positioning (2026 Context)

The AI engineering market in 2026 rewards:
- **Demonstrated systems** over described knowledge
- **Production experience** over tutorial completion
- **Agent system builders** over prompt writers
- **Eval-driven engineers** over demo builders
- **MCP integrations** over isolated scripts

Lorraine's positioning strategy:
1. Ship AI systems publicly (GitHub, blog, demos)
2. Write specifically about what she built and what she learned
3. Contribute to or extend MCP ecosystem
4. Speak / write about AI engineering from a practitioner perspective
5. Be the visible Black woman AI engineer building community AND systems

Anti-patterns to avoid:
- Generic "AI enthusiast" positioning
- Certification-only credentialing without shipped work
- Tutorial-level GitHub repositories as portfolio pieces
- Staying invisible while others with less skill position louder
